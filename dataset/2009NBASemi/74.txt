Lakers Beat Nuggets, Seek NBA Title for Second Year (Update1) 

May 30 (Bloomberg) -- Kobe Bryant scored 35 points to lead the Los Angeles Lakers to a 119-92 win over the Denver Nuggets and a spot in the National Basketball Association Finals for the second consecutive year.

The Lakers, who lost to the Boston Celtics in a 4-2 series last year, won the Western Conference finals 4-2 over the Nuggets. They will play the Orlando Magic or Cleveland Cavaliers in the finals.

Los Angeles last won the championship in 2001-2002 when the team, led by Shaquille O¡¦Neal, swept the series against the New Jersey Nets. Since 1946, the Lakers have amassed nine titles.

Pau Gasol had 20 points and 12 rebounds and Lamar Odom came off the bench to add 20 points last night at Pepsi Center in Denver. The Lakers hit 24 of 24 from the free-throw line.

Carmelo Anthony scored 25 points for the Nuggets and J.R. Smith came off the bench to add 24.

The NBA Finals begin June 4 at 9 p.m. New York time, with the first match-up being Orlando at Los Angeles or Los Angeles at Cleveland, depending upon the outcome of the Eastern Conference series. The Magic, who lead the series 3-2, play the Cavaliers tonight in Orlando.
