Microsoft Fined 290 Million Dollars

The U.S. software giant had lost at the U.S. Supreme Court against a small Canadian company who accused it of using a patent that they own, regarding the use of XML technology in Microsoft Word.

The compensation amounts to U.S. $290 million (200 million euros) for patent infringement.

American judges have decided unanimously to grant 290 million dollars to i4i company from Toronto, upholding the previous decision of a lower court.

U.S. Supreme Court Decision requires from the company created by Bill Gates to pay the highest fine ever in such a case and concludes a long process, started by the Canadian company in 2007.

Microsoft was supported in its appeal by other technology giants like Verizon and Intel, the distribution chain Wal Mart and car makers Toyota and General Motors.
